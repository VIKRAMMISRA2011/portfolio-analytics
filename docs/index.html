Risk & drawdown

Drawdown here is measured on the cumulative realized P&L curve — how far the booked record fell below its own high-water mark. It is not a NAV drawdown: the open book carries the marked-to-market risk, reported separately in 08 and never netted against the realized record.

Where the real risk sits. The realized curve's worst excursion is −₹5,903 — against a largest single open mark of −₹66,173. The trading process cuts realized losses early (largest organic realized loss −₹6,861); the risk concentrates in held positions. That asymmetry — quick to book winners, slow to exit losers — was surfaced by this pipeline and now drives a written position-review rule.
Deliberately withheld Sharpe, Sortino, return volatility and beta require a return series over a unified capital base; the combined money-weighted XIRR awaits a CDSL/demat transfer statement to resolve inter-account in-kind movements. Annualizing lot-level cash P&L into a pseudo-return would manufacture precision that the data does not support — these metrics ship when the inputs do, not before.

10

Methodology & data integrity

How every number on this page was derived — so it reads as verified analysis, not marketing. The build script asserts these ties before the page is written; a failed check fails the build.

  1. Ingest. Raw broker exports: Zerodha tradebook + P&L + fund ledger; AngelOne trades history + P&L statement; Groww order history + two P&L reports + balance statement + dividend report. Parsed with pandas; account identifiers stripped before anything is published.
  2. Reconcile. An independent FIFO matching engine reproduces Zerodha's stated realized P&L to ₹0.00. Groww's fund ledger reconciles to its order history within ₹40 after corporate-action adjustment.
  3. Corporate actions. Reliance 1:1 bonus, OLA Electric IPO allotment, Vishal Mega Mart IPO, Siemens Energy demerger and rights entitlements handled explicitly. The OLA IPO sale (+₹59,234) is absent from broker P&L (IPO cost basis) and was recovered from a ₹1.85L settlement-ledger gap.
  4. Charges bridge. The monthly cadence, lot and instrument tables run gross of Zerodha + Groww charges; AngelOne flows are statement nets. Gross realized ₹3,23,945 − Zerodha charges ₹6,305 − Groww charges ₹20,855 = ₹2,96,785 net ex-dividends; + ₹12,298 dividends (registrar report) = ₹3,09,083 headline. Total charges paid across brokers: ₹31,893.
  5. Risk metrics. Drawdown is computed on the cumulative realized P&L curve (high-water-mark basis) — labeled as such everywhere; it is not NAV drawdown. Expectancy = mean lot P&L; payoff ratio = avg win ÷ |avg loss|; profit factor = gross profits ÷ |gross losses|, reported both including and excluding the offsetting Reliance bonus legs.
  6. Benchmark. NIFTY 50 TRI tracked via a published index-fund NAV (full citation in section 09): month-end values, rebased to 100 at window start, final point month-to-date. AMFI-published NAVs are public record and track the TRI within ~0.1–0.2%/yr. Regime context only — no relative-return claim.
  7. Marks. Open positions priced from broker statements as of the dates shown per row; no model prices.
  8. Separation. Realized record and open book are presented separately by design; neither is netted into the other anywhere on this page.
Definitions
Win rate — share of FIFO lots with P&L > 0 · Expectancy — mean P&L per lot · Payoff ratio — average win ÷ |average loss| · Profit factor — Σ wins ÷ |Σ losses| · Hit rate (monthly) — share of active months with positive realized P&L · Max drawdown (realized curve) — deepest fall of cumulative realized P&L below its prior peak · Turnover — buy value + sell value.

Pending — disclosed, not hidden

  • Combined money-weighted return (XIRR) — external cash-flow set assembled from all three broker ledgers (118 flows, ledgers conserve to the paisa); withheld pending a CDSL/demat transfer statement to resolve inter-account in-kind movements before a single capital base can be struck
